Our Legacy



Investing is our family tradition. Ever since our grandfather got his first job on Wall Street, financial management has been our driving passion. Now as third-generation money managers, we fuse the lessons of the past with modern technology.

Our core philosophy

Above all, we seek positive returns with an acceptable amount of risk. We take the long view on investing and focus on the company performance behind the securities we buy-never wavering based on novelty or short-term trends.

Backed by
a force in finance

Our founder, Robert A. Podesta, was a titan of investing. After attending night school at Northwestern in the 1940s, he forced his way into the financial industry, founding and leading Cruttenden, Podesta & Co. and The Chicago Corporation. He then served on the board of governors for the American Stock Exchange. In the 1970s he led the Economic Development Administration and served as Assistant Secretary of Commerce. His insights on the underlying value of companies formed the basis of his own investment philosophy—an approach that lives on today at Podesta Capital Advisors.

Our Approach



Using our proprietary model, we examine over 5,000 companies-the entire universe of U.S.-listed equity securities-and determine the right investments based on earnings stability and upward momentum. We ignore the latest financial headlines and simply focus on the data.

Guiding Principles



Discipline

We assess a finite set of data-examining quarterly GAAP earnings reports and running the same quantitative analysis that we have for over 40 years.

Objectivity

We disregard opinion and focus on facts, with a belief that stable and upward-sloping earnings create value in a company's stock price.

Stability

Our approach aims for more stable returns and risk profiles than industry benchmarks or mere indexing. Our goal is not only superior returns, but superior risk-adjusted returns.

How it Works

As we evaluate all 5,000 stocks listed on U.S. exchanges, we run a statistical regression analysis on each company's current earnings, as well as its past earnings and earnings growth. Our model recommends which securities to buy and which to sell, with the underlying belief that stable and increasing earnings will result in price appreciation over the long term.
